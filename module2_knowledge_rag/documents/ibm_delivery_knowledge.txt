1.1 RAG Status Definitions
---------------------------
IBM uses RAG (Red/Amber/Green) status for all project reporting:

GREEN: Project is on track. All milestones met. Budget within 10% of plan.
No critical risks. Stakeholder satisfaction high.

AMBER: Project has issues requiring attention. One or more milestones at risk.
Budget variance between 10-20%. At least one high-priority risk open.
Corrective action plan in place.

RED: Project is in trouble. Multiple milestones missed. Budget variance >20%.
Critical risks with no mitigation. Immediate escalation required.

1.2 IBM Weekly Status Report Format
-------------------------------------
Every IBM project must submit a weekly status report with the following sections:

EXECUTIVE SUMMARY (2-3 sentences max):
State overall RAG status, key achievement this week, and top concern.

ACCOMPLISHMENTS THIS WEEK:
- Bullet list of completed deliverables
- Include % completion for major workstreams
- Reference milestone IDs from project plan

PLAN FOR NEXT WEEK:
- Specific tasks with owner and due date
- Highlight any dependencies on client or third parties

RISKS AND ISSUES:
Format: [ID] | [Description] | [Probability: H/M/L] | [Impact: H/M/L] | [Mitigation]
Example: R001 | Client SME unavailable for UAT | H | H | Escalate to client PM, identify backup SME

DECISIONS REQUIRED:
List any decisions needed from client or IBM leadership with deadline.

BUDGET STATUS:
Planned spend to date: $XXX,XXX
Actual spend to date: $XXX,XXX
Variance: +/- $XX,XXX (X%)
Forecast to complete: $XXX,XXX

1.3 IBM Escalation Protocol
-----------------------------
Level 1: Project Manager resolves within project team (0-2 days)
Level 2: Delivery Manager engaged (2-5 days, AMBER status)
Level 3: IBM Partner/Associate Partner engaged (5+ days, RED status)
Level 4: IBM Executive Sponsor engaged (critical project failure risk)

SECTION 2: IBM GARAGE METHODOLOGY
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

2.1 What is IBM Garage?
------------------------
IBM Garage is IBM's innovation methodology that combines Design Thinking,
Lean Startup, and Agile practices to help clients move from idea to
production-ready solution rapidly.

IBM Garage has three phases:
1. DISCOVER: Understand the problem (Design Thinking workshops, user research)
2. EXPLORE: Build and test solutions (MVPs, prototypes, user testing)
3. SCALE: Deploy and grow (production deployment, change management)

2.2 IBM Garage Principles
---------------------------
- Co-creation: IBM and client work together, not IBM delivering to client
- Fail fast: Small experiments, quick learning, rapid iteration
- Minimum Viable Product (MVP): Build the smallest thing that delivers value
- Continuous delivery: Deploy frequently, get feedback early
- Measure everything: Define success metrics before building

2.3 IBM Garage Roles
---------------------
- Garage Lead: IBM consultant who facilitates the Garage process
- Product Owner: Client representative who owns the product vision
- Squad: Cross-functional team (IBM + client) of 5-9 people
- Chapter Lead: IBM technical expert who supports multiple squads
- Site Reliability Engineer (SRE): Ensures production stability

2.4 IBM Garage Ceremonies
---------------------------
- Daily Standup: 15 minutes, what did you do, what will you do, blockers
- Sprint Planning: 2 hours, plan work for next 2-week sprint
- Sprint Review: 1 hour, demo completed work to stakeholders
- Sprint Retrospective: 1 hour, what went well, what to improve
- Backlog Refinement: 1 hour, groom and prioritize upcoming work

SECTION 3: IBM PROJECT RISK MANAGEMENT

3.1 IBM Risk Categories
------------------------
IBM classifies project risks into 6 categories:

SCOPE RISK: Requirements unclear, scope creep, changing client priorities
SCHEDULE RISK: Unrealistic timelines, resource unavailability, dependencies
BUDGET RISK: Cost overruns, currency fluctuation, unexpected expenses
RESOURCE RISK: Key person dependency, skill gaps, team attrition
TECHNICAL RISK: Technology complexity, integration challenges, performance
STAKEHOLDER RISK: Low engagement, conflicting priorities, decision delays

3.2 IBM Risk Assessment Matrix
--------------------------------
Probability × Impact = Risk Score

         | Low Impact | Medium Impact | High Impact
---------|------------|---------------|------------
High Prob| AMBER      | RED           | RED
Med Prob | GREEN      | AMBER         | RED
Low Prob | GREEN      | GREEN         | AMBER

3.3 IBM Risk Mitigation Strategies
------------------------------------
AVOID: Change project plan to eliminate the risk entirely
TRANSFER: Move risk to another party (insurance, contracts, subcontractors)
MITIGATE: Reduce probability or impact of the risk
ACCEPT: Acknowledge risk and prepare contingency plan

3.4 Common IBM Project Risks and Mitigations
----------------------------------------------
Risk: Client SME unavailability
Mitigation: Identify backup SMEs, schedule sessions 2 weeks in advance,
document decisions to reduce dependency on single person.

Risk: Scope creep
Mitigation: Formal change control process, weekly scope review,
client sign-off on all requirement changes.

Risk: Technical complexity underestimated
Mitigation: Technical spike in Sprint 1, involve IBM technical architects,
build proof-of-concept before committing to full implementation.

Risk: Key IBM resource leaving project
Mitigation: Knowledge transfer documentation, pair programming,
cross-training team members on critical components.

SECTION 4: IBM DELIVERY TOOLS AND PROCESSES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

4.1 IBM Project Management Tools
----------------------------------
- IBM ELM (Engineering Lifecycle Management): Requirements and test management
- IBM Rational DOORS: Requirements management for complex projects
- Jira: Agile project tracking (most common in IBM Garage projects)
- Microsoft Project: Traditional waterfall project planning
- IBM Connections: Team collaboration and document sharing
- Slack@IBM: Real-time team communication
- Box@IBM: Document storage and sharing with clients

4.2 IBM Time Tracking
----------------------
IBM consultants must track time daily using IBM's internal time tracking system.
- Billable time: Time charged to client project code
- Non-billable time: Internal IBM activities (training, admin, proposals)
- Target utilization: 85-90% billable for delivery consultants
- Time must be submitted by Friday 5pm each week

4.3 IBM Expense Management
----------------------------
IBM uses Concur for expense management.
- Client entertainment: Requires pre-approval for amounts >$100
- Travel: Book through IBM's preferred travel agency (BCD Travel)
- Meals: $75/day per diem for domestic travel, $100/day international
- Receipts required for all expenses >$25
- Submit expenses within 30 days of incurring

4.4 IBM Statement of Work (SOW)
---------------------------------
An SOW is the legal contract between IBM and the client. It defines:
- Scope of work and deliverables
- Timeline and milestones
- Pricing and payment terms
- Assumptions and dependencies
- Change control process
- Acceptance criteria

IBM SOW must be reviewed by IBM Legal before signing.
Any scope changes require a formal Change Order (CO) to the SOW.

- Band 8: Senior Consultant / Manager (5-10 years)
- Band 9: Associate Partner (10-15 years)
- Band 10: Partner (15+ years)

5.3 IBM Performance Review Process
-------------------------------------
IBM uses a continuous performance management approach:
- Quarterly check-ins with manager
- Year-end performance rating (1-5 scale)
- Promotion decisions made annually in Q4
- Key factors: Client feedback, delivery quality, IBM values, skills growth

SECTION 6: IBM INTERN PROGRAM — DELIVERY CONSULTING TRACK

6.1 IBM Intern Expectations
-----------------------------
IBM interns in the delivery consulting track are expected to:
- Contribute to real client projects from Week 1
- Complete assigned IBM digital badges within internship period
- Present a final project to IBM leadership at end of internship
- Maintain 85%+ attendance in all IBM training sessions
- Submit weekly status updates to manager

6.2 IBM Intern to Full-Time Conversion
----------------------------------------
IBM evaluates interns for full-time offers based on:
- Technical skills demonstrated (30%)
- Client/project delivery quality (30%)
- IBM values alignment (20%)
- Manager recommendation (20%)

Interns who receive full-time offers typically:
- Completed 2+ IBM digital badges
- Received positive client feedback
- Demonstrated initiative beyond assigned tasks
- Built strong relationships with IBM team members

6.3 IBM Intern Final Project Requirements
------------------------------------------
The final project must:
- Solve a real business problem (not a toy example)
- Use technologies relevant to IBM's current focus areas (AI, Cloud, Data)
- Include a working demo
- Have clear business value articulation
- Be presented to a panel of IBM managers

7.1 IBM watsonx Overview
--------------------------
IBM watsonx is IBM's AI and data platform consisting of three components:
- watsonx.ai: AI studio for building, training, and deploying AI models
- watsonx.data: Data store built on open lakehouse architecture
- watsonx.governance: AI governance and risk management platform

7.2 watsonx.ai Key Features
-----------------------------
- Foundation model library (Llama, Granite, Mistral, etc.)
- Prompt Lab for prompt engineering
- AutoAI for automated model building
- Model evaluation and comparison tools
- API access for integration with applications

7.3 IBM Granite Models
-----------------------
IBM Granite is IBM's family of open-source foundation models:
- Granite-3B-Code: Code generation and completion
- Granite-7B-Instruct: General instruction following
- Granite-13B-Chat: Conversational AI
- Granite-34B-Code: Advanced code generation

IBM Granite models are available on watsonx.ai and HuggingFace.
