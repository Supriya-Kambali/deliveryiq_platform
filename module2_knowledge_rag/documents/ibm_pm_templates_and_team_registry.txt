IBM DELIVERY TEAM ROLES — WHO DOES WHAT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

DELIVERY MANAGER (DM)
Seniority: Typically Senior or Principal
Owns: Overall program delivery, client relationship, budget, risk escalation
Responsibilities:
- Accountable for delivery quality, timelines, and client satisfaction
- Manages the overall risk register; escalates to partner level when needed
- Owns the statement of work (SOW) and change request process
- Conducts weekly/bi-weekly steering committee meetings with client sponsors
- Reviews and approves weekly status reports before they go to clients
- Manages resource allocation across squads on multi-squad programs
IBM tool: IBM Garage Experience platform for value tracking

ENGAGEMENT LEAD / PROGRAM LEAD
Seniority: Mid-level to Senior
Owns: Day-to-day delivery coordination, squad coordination
Responsibilities:
- Facilitates cross-squad dependency management
- Owns the integrated delivery plan and milestone tracking
- Escalation point for squad-level blockers that cannot be resolved internally
- Prepares the weekly status summary for Delivery Manager review
- Coordinates with client project managers on schedules

SQUAD LEAD / SCRUM MASTER
Seniority: Mid-level
Owns: Sprint delivery, agile ceremonies, team impediments
Responsibilities:
- Facilitates all Scrum ceremonies (planning, standup, review, retro)
- Removes blockers identified in daily standups
- Tracks sprint velocity and burndown
- Escalates blockers to Engagement Lead if unresolved after 48 hours
- Coaches client team members on Agile practices
IBM certification: Enterprise Design Thinking Practitioner recommended

PRODUCT OWNER (PO)
Seniority: Senior
Owns: Product backlog, requirements priority, stakeholder alignment
Responsibilities:
- Runs the Funnel process: stakeholder workshops → prioritized backlog
- Enforces the Firewall: no scope changes mid-sprint without PO approval
- Writes and refines user stories with acceptance criteria
- Runs backlog refinement sessions
- Prioritizes bugs vs. features based on business value
- Owns the product roadmap (Outcome-Based Roadmap format)

ARCHITECT / TECHNICAL LEAD
Seniority: Senior to Principal
Owns: Technical decisions, architecture quality, non-functional requirements
Responsibilities:
- Defines and enforces the reference architecture
- Reviews all design decisions; raises architecture risks
- Ensures non-functional requirements (performance, security, scalability) are
  addressed in sprint planning
- Pairs with developers on complex technical stories
- Presents architecture to client technical stakeholders

DEVELOPER (Mid-level / Senior)
Owns: Story implementation, code quality, unit testing
Responsibilities:
- Estimates and implements user stories
- Writes unit tests for all new code
- Participates in pair programming sessions
- Raises technical blockers in daily standup
- Reviews peers' code in pull requests
- Contributes to backlog refinement with effort estimates

QA / TEST ENGINEER
Seniority: Junior to Mid-level
Owns: Test coverage, defect management, quality gates
Responsibilities:
- Writes test plans and test cases for each user story
- Executes regression testing before Sprint Review
- Manages defect backlog: prioritizes with PO
- Tracks defect escape rate
- Validates acceptance criteria before marking stories Done

DESIGNER / UX RESEARCHER
Seniority: Mid-level
Owns: User experience, design system, Sponsor User engagement
Responsibilities:
- Conducts user research (contextual inquiry, interviews) in the Think phase
- Creates wireframes and prototypes for developer handoff
- Facilitates Design Thinking workshops (Hills, Playbacks)
- Manages Sponsor User relationships and research sessions
- Ensures designs meet accessibility standards

WEEKLY STATUS REPORT — IBM FORMAT
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

IBM standard weekly status report sections:

1. OVERALL STATUS
   - RAG (Red/Amber/Green) overall
   - One-sentence summary of the week

2. MILESTONES
   - Key milestones this reporting period and their status
   - Upcoming milestones next period

3. ACCOMPLISHMENTS THIS WEEK
   - Completed user stories or deliverables
   - Key decisions made
   - Metrics achieved

4. ISSUES AND RISKS (RAID Log extract)
   Risks:
   ID | Description | Likelihood | Impact | Owner | Mitigation | Due Date
   Issues:
   ID | Description | Impact | Owner | Resolution Plan | Due Date

5. DECISIONS REQUIRED
   - What needs to be decided by the client
   - Owner and deadline for each decision

6. PLAN FOR NEXT WEEK
   - Sprint goal (if applicable)
   - Key activities planned
   - Dependencies expected from client

7. FINANCIAL SUMMARY (for Delivery Manager version)
   - Budget consumed to date vs. plan
   - Forecast to complete
   - Variance explanation if off-track

RISK MANAGEMENT — IBM DELIVERY FRAMEWORK
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

IBM uses a RAID log for delivery risk management:
R = Risks (things that might happen)
A = Assumptions (things we're treating as true without confirmation)
I = Issues (problems that have already occurred)
D = Dependencies (things we need from other parties to proceed)

RISK CLASSIFICATION:
Likelihood: High (>70% likely) / Medium (30-70%) / Low (<30%)
Impact: High (project-threatening) / Medium (delivery delay) / Low (manageable)

RISK SCORING:
High Likelihood + High Impact = Critical — escalate immediately to DM
High Likelihood + Medium Impact = High — mitigation plan required this sprint
Medium + Medium = Medium — monitor weekly
Low + Any = Low — log and review monthly

RISK RESPONSE TYPES:
- MITIGATE: Take action to reduce likelihood or impact
- ACCEPT: Acknowledge risk and monitor; no action taken
- TRANSFER: Shift risk ownership to another party (client, vendor)
- AVOID: Change the plan to eliminate the risk

IBM escalation rule: Any Critical risk must be verbally communicated to the
Delivery Manager within 24 hours of identification, before it appears in the
written status report.

STAKEHOLDER COMMUNICATION TEMPLATES
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

DELAY NOTIFICATION EMAIL — IBM FORMAT
Subject: [PROJECT NAME] — Schedule Update: [SPECIFIC MILESTONE]
Structure:
1. What is delayed and by how long (be specific — don't hedge)
2. Root cause (factual, no blame)
3. Impact on downstream milestones or go-live
4. Mitigation actions already taken
5. What is needed from the client (if anything)
6. Next update timing

IBM rule: Never send a delay notification without also providing the
mitigation plan. "We are delayed" without "here's what we're doing about it"
is never acceptable in IBM client communication.

ESCALATION EMAIL — IBM FORMAT
Subject: [PROJECT NAME] — Escalation Required: [TOPIC]
Structure:
1. Issue summary in 2 sentences maximum
2. Business impact of the issue
3. Decision or action needed — from whom, by when
4. What happens if no decision is made by the deadline
5. IBM recommendation

IBM rule: Escalations should go through the correct channel.
Consultant → Engagement Lead → Delivery Manager → Partner → Client Sponsor.
Bypassing levels creates relationship damage. Only skip levels in genuine
emergencies.

SCOPE CHANGE REQUEST — IBM FORMAT
When a client requests work outside the SOW:
1. Acknowledge the request positively
2. Confirm it falls outside current scope (cite specific SOW section)
3. Estimate impact on timeline and budget
4. Submit formal Change Request document for approval
5. Do not begin out-of-scope work until CR is signed

IBM rule: Never agree to scope changes verbally or over email without a
signed Change Request. This protects both IBM and the client.

PROJECT HEALTH SCORING — HOW IBM DELIVERY IQ CALCULATES RISK
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━

IBM DeliveryIQ uses a machine learning model to calculate project health
scores (0-100) and risk levels (Low/Medium/High) based on:

HIGH RISK indicators (major weight in model):
- Schedule variance > 15%
- Budget variance > 10%
- Unresolved critical risks or issues > 2
- Stakeholder engagement score < 50
- Team size < 3 or > 15
- Sprint velocity declining 3+ sprints in a row

MEDIUM RISK indicators:
- Team experience score (< 3 years average)
- Requirements completeness < 70%
- Test coverage < 60%
- Client availability score (sponsor not engaged)

LOW RISK indicators (protective factors):
- Clear executive sponsor with weekly engagement
- Defined go-live criteria agreed with client
- Automated CI/CD pipeline in place
- Dedicated QA resources (not shared with dev)
- Formal risk review cadence (weekly)

Health score interpretation:
80-100: Green — on track, routine monitoring
60-79: Amber — attention needed, escalate blockers
40-59: Red — intervention required, DM must be informed
0-39: Critical — emergency escalation, partner involvement likely needed
